# Marketing & Messaging Ideas

## Core Value Proposition

Share a project, recipient runs it with zero setup.

## Tagline Ideas

### Inverting the meme
- "Works on their machine"
- "It works on everyone's machine"

### Simplicity angle
- "Share projects, not setup instructions"
- "Send a project. They click run."
- "One file. Zero setup."

### Pain point direct
- "Stop debugging their environment"
- "READMEs lie. This doesn't."

## Positioning by Audience

### For freelancers/agencies
"Deliver work your clients can actually run. No more support calls about Node versions."

### For educators
"Students run your code on day one. Not day three after fighting with Python paths."

### For open source
"Let people try your project before they commit to cloning it."

## Demo/Launch Angles

### The 30-second video
Show exporting a project, sending the file, recipient double-clicks, it runs. No terminal. No instructions.

### The "fresh Mac" stunt
Literally buy a new Mac, unbox it on camera, import a .ouijit file, watch a full-stack app run.

### Comparison content
"What it takes to run a project" - show the 15-step README vs the one-click Ouijit import.

## Name Thoughts

"Ouijit" is fun (widget + ouija?) but might be hard to spell/search. Could lean into it ("let the project speak for itself") or consider if it's serving you.

## Questions to Answer

- What audience to target first?
- What's the distribution strategy? (direct download, word of mouth, ProductHunt, HN?)
- Free vs paid? Freemium?
- What types of projects resonate most? (web apps, scripts, full-stack?)
